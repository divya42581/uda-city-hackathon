Executive Summary

The future +2.5 C pseudo-warming scenario increases dangerous-heat hours across every neighbourhood. However, the highest physical heat hazard is not always the highest final heat risk because risk also depends on exposure and vulnerability.

Kampong Lama Highest final heat risk in both present and future scenarios.
Jade Gardens Highest heat hazard, but low final risk because daytime exposure is low.
+162 hours Average increase in dangerous-heat hours across the 10 neighbourhoods.

Main interpretation: planning should not be based on heat hazard alone. The priority areas are where heat hazard overlaps with high population exposure and social vulnerability.

How To Read This Report

  • Heat hazard is the number of hours where hourly mean SUEWS 2 m air temperature is above 35 C.
  • Exposure is represented by daytime population density. A physically hot area with low daytime exposure can still have low final risk.
  • Vulnerability combines proxy indicators including age structure, low AC access, outdoor workers, and deprivation.
  • Final risk combines hazard, exposure, and vulnerability using a geometric mean. Results are relative rankings within UDA-city, not absolute health-outcome predictions.
